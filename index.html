FAQ

Common
questions.

Everything you need to know before booking your driveway sealcoating.

Still have questions? →
How long does the whole process take?
Most driveways are completed in 1–2 hours from start to finish. This includes surface cleaning, crack filling, edge masking, sealcoat application, and blocking off the area. Your driveway will be ready to drive on by the next morning.
When can I drive on my driveway after sealing?
We recommend waiting 24 hours before driving on the sealed surface. We'll block off the driveway with cones and give you a complete dry-time briefing so you know exactly when it's safe.
How often should I sealcoat my driveway?
For optimal protection, we recommend sealcoating every 2–3 years depending on traffic and exposure. Our premium sealers are designed to last longer than standard products, so many of our clients go 3 full years between coats.
What products do you use?
We use only commercial-grade asphalt emulsion sealers — the same products used on municipal roads and parking lots. No diluted or consumer-grade products. Our crack fillers are professional-grade rubberized compounds that flex with temperature changes.
Do you guarantee your work?
Absolutely. Every job comes with our satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the result, we'll come back and make it right — no questions asked. We also provide before and after photos for your records.
What if it rains on the day of my appointment?
We monitor the weather closely and will reschedule if rain is expected within 24 hours. Sealcoat needs dry conditions to cure properly, and we won't compromise quality. We'll contact you the day before to confirm or reschedule.
